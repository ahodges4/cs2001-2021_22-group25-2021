Privacy policy and copyright statement

Our approach to privacy

This website collects data on the admins and charitiy foodbanks using the website. The use of this data is governed by data protection laws. In the UK these laws are the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK-GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

This page sets out how Food4Everyone complies with the legislation, your rights about how your data is managed and how you can contact us if you have questions.

Food4U is made by Brunel University students with the help of staff, this means the data this website collects is not only controlled by Food4Everyone but also Brunel University. This means we both make decisions about what data we collect from you, and us at Food4Everyone decide how we use it in line with the law.

We won’t do anything with your information you wouldn’t reasonably expect.

To keep your data secure we encrypt all passwords used using a hash key specially designed for our website, this enusures protection from hackers and protects against information leaks.

We store any data we recieve on our onsite database and only disclose inforamtion to official members of the Food4Everyone website and authorised members of the Brunel Universty staff.

We keep the data of verified Food4Everyone users permanantly unless said member wants to remove their data. Please refer to givefood.orgs privacy policy to know the record retention of the data in the foodbank.org API.

When we process your personal data, we need to have a lawful basis to do so. A lawful basis is a reason in law that permits us to use your data for a specific purpose. The lawful basis we use depends on what we are doing with your data (our purpose for having it). We have summarised our key lawful basis and our purpose for having your data below.

The reporting of data to regulatory or statutory bodies including the Office for Students, HESA, NSS, etc.

The collection and use of data to ensure we comply with the Equality Act 2010. The collection and use of data that is required to ensure compliance with Health & Safety legislation.The collection and use of data to ensure compliance with financial, taxation and pensions legislation.The collection and use of data to ensure compliance with immigration legislation and terms of our UKVI licencing conditions.

When you want us to discuss your queries with someone else such as a relative or guardian. When you want us to share your details with a 3rd party so they can contact you.

In the UK everyone has rights about how their personal data is managed. The rights are:

The right to be informed

The right of access

The right to rectification

The right to erasure

The right to restrict processing

The right to data portability

The right to object

Rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling.

You can make an information rights request at any time by contacting our data protection team via email:Food4Everyone@gmx.co.uk

Concerns

if you are not happy with how we handle your data please email Food4Everyone@gmx.co.uk if you are not satisfied with our response you have the right to make a complaint to the Information Commissioners Office. You can find out more about your rights under data protection legislation from the Information Commissioner's Office website available at: www.ico.org.uk
